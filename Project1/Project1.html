SOCIAL ENGINEERING

Cyber Criminals can use various social engineering techniques to trick people into revealing private information. Social Engineering does not exploit technology.

PHISING

Avoid phishing by ignoring suspicious emails and messages. Use two factor authentication for further protection.

RANSOMWARE

Ransomware locks a device and unlocks upon paying the ransom. Back up files and donâ€™t click suspicious links .

DUMPSTER DIVING

Dumpster diving is sifting through garbage containers or dumpsters to retrieve information that could be valuable or exploited. Always shred important documents

SHOULDER SURFING

Be careful when inputting sensitive information. A shoulder surfer can be around you logging your keystrokes for information.

SCAREWARE

Do not be tricked by fake messages. Avoid pop-ups that trick you to installing junk or malware.

BAITING

Watch out for false advertisements. Cyber Criminals can try to steal your data through false advertisements.

PRETEXTING

Pretexting is impersonation and fabrication of a story to extort data or money. Verify a caller's identity before revealing sensitive information.

TAILGATING

Watch out for tailgaters. People can infiltrate facilities and steal information, such as passwords on a sticky note, if you let them in.

What is the Importance of Cybersecurity?

  • Protecting Personal Information: Cybersecurity keeps your personal data safe from hackers.
  • Safeguarding Finances: It helps prevent unauthorized access to your financial accounts.
  • Ensuring Online Safety: Cybersecurity measures keep you safe by blocking harmful malware.
  • Maintaining Privacy: To keep online activities and communications confidential.

How to Protect Data

  1. Be Cautious with Links and Attachments.
  2. Use Strong Passwords.
  3. Enable Two-Factor Authentication.
  4. Keep Software Updated.
